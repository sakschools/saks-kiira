Today has been an exciting and memorable day at Sir Apollo Kaggwa Primary School–Kira as the entire school community came together to celebrate DEAR Day under the inspiring theme “Read Your Way.” The school compound was alive with energy, enthusiasm, and a shared sense of purpose as learners, teachers, parents, and guests joined hands to highlight the importance of reading in the academic and personal development of every child. 

This celebration reflected the school’s commitment to holistic education, which emphasizes not only academic excellence but also talent development, creativity, character formation, and the nurturing of lifelong skills that prepare learners to excel in all aspects of life.

![](/img/1001522259.jpg)

The DEAR Day provided a meaningful opportunity for learners to pause their regular classroom activities and fully immerse themselves in the world of books. DEAR, which stands for “Drop Everything and Read,” encouraged pupils from different classes to eagerly explore a wide variety of storybooks, imaginative narratives, and informative texts. These reading experiences not only strengthened literacy and comprehension skills but also fostered creativity, critical thinking, and emotional growth, essential elements of the school’s holistic approach, which combines academic excellence with the development of individual talents and personal expression.

A major highlight of the celebration was the presence of Dr. Sylvia Nalubwama from Makerere University, who graced the occasion as the Chief Reader. Her participation greatly enriched the event as she engaged learners during the reading sessions, sharing inspiring reflections about the transformative power of books. Dr. Nalubwama emphasized that reading is a cornerstone for academic achievement, creativity, self-confidence, and lifelong learning, and encouraged learners to explore stories that ignite their imagination and individual talents.

![](/img/1001522324.jpg)

Her interactive session created an atmosphere of excitement and motivation, demonstrating to learners that reading is not just a classroom activity but a gateway to personal growth, exploration of talents, and discovery of new ideas. By participating directly, she exemplified how embracing reading and learning can help young people develop their skills, nurture their unique abilities, and prepare for success beyond school.

![](/img/1001522313.jpg)

The celebration was further enhanced by the active involvement of parents and guardians, whose presence created a warm, supportive environment. Parents participated alongside learners in reading activities, reinforcing the school’s message that cultivating literacy and encouraging talent development is a shared responsibility. Their engagement demonstrated that reading and learning can be a collaborative, family-centered experience that nurtures the potential in every child.

Among the many memorable moments of the day was a delightful showcase of the main characters from nursery storybooks. Learners confidently presented the familiar characters, bringing stories to life in a way that sparked curiosity, imagination, and peer engagement. This activity not only strengthened reading comprehension but also allowed learners to explore their creative talents, highlighting the school’s commitment to integrating talent development into every aspect of holistic education.

![](/img/1001522316.jpg)

Storytelling sessions also added vibrancy to the celebration. Learners and parents listened attentively as captivating narratives unfolded, encouraging imagination and active participation. One particularly touching moment saw a mother following keenly as her daughter read a story, reflecting the strong support and guidance families provide in nurturing reading habits. Such interactions exemplified how holistic education fosters academic, emotional, and creative growth simultaneously.

![](/img/1001522320.jpg)

The day also featured heartwarming family moments, including a mother and grandmother reading together with a young boy, illustrating how reading can unite generations while inspiring learners to appreciate storytelling and develop their talents. Parents further contributed by narrating their favorite stories, creating interactive and memorable moments that celebrated creativity, communication skills, and the joy of learning.

![](/img/1001522315.jpg)

![](/img/1001522314.jpg)

The leadership of the school also played an active role in the celebrations. The Headteacher participated in the reading exercises alongside the learners, modeling a culture of engagement, curiosity, and appreciation for literacy. This involvement reinforced the school’s commitment to holistic education, showing that nurturing talents, encouraging creativity, and building character are as important as academic achievement.

![](/img/1001522250.jpg)

As the day drew to a close, DEAR Day left a lasting impression on the entire school community. With the inspiring presence of Dr. Nalubwama, the enthusiastic participation of parents, and the active engagement of learners, the event successfully captured the spirit of the theme “Read Your Way.” It was a celebration of reading, imagination, and learning that fully reflected Sir Apollo Kaggwa Primary School–Kira’s dedication to holistic education, preparing learners who are not only academically competent but also creative, talented, confident, and ready to thrive in every aspect of life.
